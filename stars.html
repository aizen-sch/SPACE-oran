Stars in the Universe Stars are burning balls of gas composed primarily of hydrogen and helium, generating their energy from nuclear reactions. Their life begins within nebulae, driven by gravity, and their life cycles vary depending on their mass. Small stars end up as white dwarfs, while massive ones explode as supernovae, leaving behind neutron stars or black holes. Stars vary between dwarfs, giants, neutron stars, and black holes. They play a fundamental role in providing light and heat, and in forming the basic elements for life. Studying them helps us understand the universe and its origins.
